Personal Profile

Hengyu Gu, a native of Guangzhou, Guangdong Province, is a researcher, assistant professor and doctoral supervisor at the School of Geography and Marine Science, Nanjing University. D. in Peking University-University of Chicago joint training. His main research interests include population geography and regional development, urban computing and urban spatial governance. He has published more than 80 papers in SSCI/SCI/CSSCI, including more than 50 papers in Annals of the AAG and Journal of Geography as the first/corresponding author. He is a member of the Population Geography Committee of the Chinese Geographical Society, a council member of the China Association for the Study of Africa, a member of the International Editorial Board of Population, Space and Place, the flagship journal of international population geography, a member of the editorial board of the Spatial Demography and Population Governance series, and a member of the editorial board of Spatial Demography, Geography and Population Governance. He is also a member of the editorial board/young editorial board of Spatial Demography, Geography and Sustainability, Geography and Geographic Information Science, Geographical Research and Development, World Geography Studies, and Tropical Geography. He has published two monographs and one translation. He has been selected for the Young Talent Support Project of China Association for Science and Technology (CAST), and is the chair of the Youth Fund of National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC), Lincoln Institute China Program International Fellowship, Humanities and Social Sciences Fund of the Ministry of Education (MOE), China Merchants Charity Foundation/China Population Welfare Foundation Young Scholars Funding Program, and the Youth Program of High-end Science and Technology Innovation Think Tank of CAST. Innovation Think Tank Youth Program of China Association for Science and Technology. He has been awarded with national level approval for his policy reports, excellent doctoral dissertation by Peking University, and outstanding graduate by Beijing Municipal Government.
